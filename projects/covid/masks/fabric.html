Fabric without Filter



Makeshift masks effectiveness is based on selection of material and number of layers of fabric. When selecting material, a trade-off must be made between filtration efficiency and breathability.




    COMMON HOUSEHOLD MATERIALS:

    Gold standard protection:
  • Flannel pajamas
  • HEPA vaccuum bags (non-fiberglass)
  • Car shop towels
    Adequate protection:
  • T-shirts
  • Scarves and bandannas
  • Polypropylene non-woven fibers
  • Tea towels
  • Pillow cases
  • Underwear

    TIPS

  • Use the light test: Hold the fabric or mask up to the light and see how much light gets through. The tighter the weave, the less light you’ll see, and the more protection you’ll get. Then test the fabric over your face to make sure you can still breathe through it.
  • Coverage: should reach above the bridge of the nose and below the chin.
  • Fit: should be snug.
